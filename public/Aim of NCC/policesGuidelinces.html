Policies & Guidelines for NCC

  1. General Conduct Guidelines
    • Discipline: All NCC cadets are expected to maintain the highest level of discipline at all times, whether during training or while attending any NCC event.
    • Punctuality: Cadets must be on time for all activities, including parades, training sessions, and official events.
    • Respect: Show respect towards your seniors, fellow cadets, and instructors. Proper behavior and cooperation are essential.
    • Dress Code:Cadets should always be in the correct NCC uniform during NCC activities. The uniform represents unity, discipline, and the values of NCC.

  2. Attendance Policy
    • Regular Participation: Cadets must attend a minimum of 75% of parades and training sessions to be eligible for NCC certificates and promotions.
    • Leave of Absence: If a cadet cannot attend a session, they must inform the Associate NCC Officer (ANO) in advance, providing a valid reason.
    • Consequences of Low Attendance: Failure to meet the attendance requirements may lead to disqualification from NCC activities, camps, or future promotions.

  3. NCC Activities & Camps
    • Annual Training Camps: All cadets are encouraged to participate in ATCs (Annual Training Camps). These camps are a great way to experience outdoor activities, leadership training, and physical challenges.
    • Specialized Camps: Selected cadets may attend Republic Day Camps (RDC), Thal Sainik Camps (TSC), National Integration Camps (NIC) ,Advance Leadership Camp (ALC) based on their performance and dedication.
    • Adventure Activities: Opportunities for trekking, mountaineering, and other adventure activities will be provided. These experiences help build confidence, teamwork, and resilience.

  4. Eligibility for Promotions & Awards
    • Promotions: Cadets who demonstrate leadership, discipline, and consistent performance in both training and camps may be promoted to higher ranks (e.g., Lance Corporal, Corporal, Sergeant, etc.).
    • Certificates: Cadets can earn the following certificates based on their participation and achievements:
      • 'A' Certificate: For Junior Division (school level).
      • 'B' Certificate: For Senior Division (college level).
      • 'C' Certificate: The highest-level certificate, recognized for higher education and employment opportunities.
    • Awards: Outstanding cadets may receive awards for performance, leadership, and excellence in various fields. Awards could be at the Unit Level, State Level, or National Level.

  5. Health & Safety
    • Physical Fitness: NCC cadets are required to maintain good physical fitness. Regular physical training sessions will be conducted, and cadets are expected to participate fully.
    • Medical Check-ups: Before joining any camp or adventure activity, cadets will undergo a medical examination to ensure they are fit to participate.
    • Safety Measures: Safety is a top priority during all NCC activities. Cadets must follow instructions carefully, especially during physical training, camps, and adventure activities.

  6. Code of Conduct on Social Media
    • Positive Representation: Cadets are encouraged to represent the NCC and LPU positively on social media platforms. Avoid sharing any content that may damage the reputation of the organization.
    • Official Platforms: For any official announcements or information, refer to the LPU-NCC social media handles or the official website.
    • Cyber Safety: Cadets should follow safe practices online, avoiding the sharing of personal or sensitive information.

  7. Penalties for Misconduct
    • Minor Infractions: Small issues like tardiness or improper uniform may result in a verbal warning from the ANO or unit in-charge.
    • Serious Offenses: In cases of serious misconduct (e.g., fighting, disobedience, or damage to property), cadets may face suspension or expulsion from the NCC program. The unit will handle these cases as per NCC rules.

  8. Exit Policy
    • Voluntary Withdrawal: If a cadet wishes to leave the NCC program, they must submit a formal request to the ANO, explaining the reason for withdrawal.
    • Involuntary Removal: Cadets may be removed from the NCC program if they fail to comply with the NCC rules, maintain attendance, or show disciplinary issues.

  9. Financial Guidelines
    • Government-Aided Institutions: For cadets in government-aided institutions, most expenses related to uniforms, camps, and activities are covered by the government.
    • Self-Financed Institutions: Cadets from private or self-financed institutions may be required to bear the cost of certain activities or uniforms, as per NCC regulations.
    • No Hidden Costs: All financial obligations are clearly communicated to cadets, and no hidden charges will be imposed.
